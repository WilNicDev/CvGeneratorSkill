Myreas is the enterprise architecture division of Colruyt Group, a Belgian multi-brand group operating across food retail (Colruyt, OKay, Cru, Bio-Planet), online pharmacy (Newpharma), fitness (Jims), baby & kids (DreamBaby), and other consumer services. The Aladdin DCS programme was the group's strategic initiative to establish the Xtra loyalty card as the single identity and engagement cornerstone binding this multi-brand consumer ecosystem — giving Colruyt Group a unified view of the customer across all its brands and enabling personalised, cross-brand services. Part of a team of 50+ Enterprise Architects, reporting to the Lead IT Enterprise Architect (direct CEO report).

## Responsibilities

- IT enterprise architect working in partnership with Business Enterprise Architects for  
	- Domain digital ecosystem: IT architecture for the Aladdin DCS programme, establishing the Xtra card as the cornerstone of a unified consumer ecosystem spanning most Colruyt Group brands and service categories (food retail, pharmacy, fitness, and more) — enabling a single customer identity and cross-brand engagement at group level.
	- Domain IT: ensuring pragmatic alignment of CG IT organisation with ITIL and IT4IT frameworks in combination with ServiceNow
- Core team member of Aladin DCS program.
## Key Achievements

- Led IT architecture governance and solution design for DCS program: architecture team included members from CG and from the technology consortium selected for the program.
- Led IT architecture governance for the Aladdin DCS programme: a group-level strategic initiative to establish the Xtra card as the single customer identity across Colruyt Group's portfolio of brands (food retail, online pharmacy, fitness, baby & kids, and other consumer services), enabling a unified view of the customer and cross-brand personalisation at scale.
- Standardisation of ServiceNow implementation
- Extension of serviceNow beyond IT service management: implementation of case management pilot (handling of product complaints within the scope of food health regulations).
- Alignment of IT processes with IT4IT framework
